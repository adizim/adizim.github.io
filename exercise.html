Fitness

I spend a lot of time exercising or reading about fitness. The activities that I have stuck to over the years are running, swimming, basketball, and weightlifting. I try to maintain a balanced split of cardiovascular training and weightlifting every week, though I usually lean more towards weightlifting.

My current routine involves a PPL weightlifting program (push/pull/legs), low intensity running, and sprints.

My favorite resources for fitness literature and knowledge, in no particular order

  • r/fitness: One stop shop for everything fitness related from diet to exercise.
  • Stronger by Science Guides: The best guides on the "Big Three" weightlifts, which are deadlift, squat, and bench.
  • Joe Defranco's "Limber 11": The best flexibility routine. [gif]
  • Foundation Training for Lower Back: Fantastic lower back routine that fixes pain and posture issues. Be warned, it is very difficult!
  • Antranik: Bodyweight exercises and stretching.
  • Jeff Nippard: Science-based fitness and weightlifting analysis.
