My Music "Career"

In the 5th grade, I started playing the clarinet. That simple decision has led to some of the greatest experiences of my life.

In the band scene, I was able to receieve 1st chair in the top group at California All-State for five consecutive years. I also accompanied Homestead High School's top band to a stunning tour in Eastern Europe.

In middle school, I was introduced to symphony through the El Camino Youth Symphony (now called Golden State Youth Symphony), and it became a central activity in my life. I was awarded a couple concertos with the group and went on an amazing tour with them too. In the junior year of high school, I earned one of four clarinet positions in the distinguished San Francisco Symphony Youth Orchestra. Playing in the historic Davies Symphony Hall every Saturday was unforgettable.

My journey brought me to the farthest corners of the world to play in fancy halls with close friends. These times constitute my fondest memories. Above all, I learned the value of deeply honing a craft and the satisfaction that progress can bring. I strive for the same level of commitment in developing other crafts such as programming.

An eternally grateful thank you goes to my parents and my teacher, Debra Gardner, for their unwavering support and guidance.
